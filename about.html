Short bio (tl;dr)

I am an Austrian researcher and curator based in Dundee, Scotland, where I work as Reader in Computational Arts & Technology at Abertay University. My work focuses on artistic and activist experiments with emerging technologies such as AI and blockchain, intellectual property issues in contemporary art, and aspects of experimental videogame culture.

I published the monograph Tactical Entanglements: AI Art, Creative Agency, and the Limits of Intellectual Property (meson press, 2021, Open Access), and Structures of Belonging (Aksioma Postscriptum Series, 2023) on re-imagining blockchain technologies beyond digital property enclosures. I co-curated Vector Festival (Toronto, 2014–2020) and led the 2018 MoneyLab symposium (London). Writing appears in books such as Artists Re:Thinking the Blockchain and the MoneyLab Reader 2, in journals including Leonardo, Philosophy & Technology, Culture Machine, and Media Theory, and in art magazines such as Spike Art Magazine and Outland.

Longer bio

After years in Vienna, Toronto, and London, I am now based in Dundee, Scotland, holding a research and teaching role as Reader in Computational Media at Abertay University. My work spans artistic and activist experiments with AI and blockchain, digital art, appropriation-based practices, cultural ownership and IP, political economies of new media, experimental videogame culture, and more-than-human agency.

A common thread in my research is how concepts like authorship, creativity, originality, agency, and ownership are reconfigured in emerging tech contexts; how they become legally or algorithmically encoded in service of capital; and how artist/activist communities respond critically. My monograph Tactical Entanglements (meson press, 2021) pulls many of these interests together. Recent contributions to discourse on creative AI and non-human agency include work in Chimeras: Inventory of Synthetic Cognition (Onassis Foundation, 2022). My blockchain writing includes Structures of Belonging (Aksioma Postscriptum Series, 2023).

Additional publications appear in Meta.space (Distanz Verlag, 2022), the MoneyLab Reader Vol. 2 (Institute of Network Cultures, 2018), Artists Re:Thinking the Blockchain (Torque Editions, 2017), Sampling Media (Oxford University Press, 2014), and in journals such as Leonardo (2023), Culture Machine (2021), Media Theory (2019), and Philosophy & Technology (2018). My practice has also featured in art publications including Spike Art Magazine and Outland.

I frequently speak at international art organisations and festivals focused on new media and technology (Aksioma, BEK, Uroboros Festival, Zentrum für Netzkunst, among others). In curatorial work, I co-directed/co-curated Vector Festival (Toronto, 2014–2020). I also advise artists and creative practitioners on IP questions (often around fair dealing/fair use) and have served as consultant and/or expert witness on legal cases.
