This essay reframes blockchains as shared civic and cultural infrastructure rather than property rails. Drawing on DAOs, cooperativism, and mixed on/off-chain practices, it argues for “structures of belonging” that foreground collaboration, mutual care, and co-ownership over speculation.

Case studies look at community-led governance experiments, artist-led token-gating as hospitality instead of exclusion, and hybrid archives that keep provenance public without enclosing cultural memory. The piece critiques extractive NFT logics and instead sketches how blockchains might sustain collective rights and responsibilities.
